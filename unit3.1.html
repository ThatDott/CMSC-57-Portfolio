Learnings

Graph representation involves using mathematical structures like adjacency matrices and lists to depict relationships between objects. These methods help us visualize and analyze how different elements are connected. Graph isomorphism checks if two graphs are structurally identical despite having different labels or arrangements. This means it can determine if two seemingly different graphs actually have the same underlying structure.

Reflection

Understanding graph representation and isomorphism helps recognize similarities and differences in complex structures, exploring the essence of connectivity. By examining how elements are connected and identifying structurally identical graphs, we gain insights into the underlying patterns and relationships.

Application

These concepts are fundamental in computer science for tasks related to networks, in chemistry for molecular structure analysis, and in social networks for information retrieval and pattern recognition.
