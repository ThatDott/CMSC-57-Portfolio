Learnings

Probability theory is essential for understanding and quantifying chance and uncertainty. It starts with basic concepts such as sample space and events. The sample space is the set of all possible outcomes in a situation, like heads or tails in a coin flip. Events are specific outcomes within this space, such as getting heads. As we delve deeper, we encounter probability distributions, which show the likelihood of different outcomes, expectation, which is the average outcome over many repetitions, and variance, which measures how much outcomes vary from the expectation.

Reflection

Understanding probability theory changes how we think about uncertainty. It makes us question our assumptions and encourages us to think more carefully when making decisions. By considering the likelihood of different outcomes, we can make more thoughtful and informed choices.

Application

Probability theory is widely used in risk management, medical diagnosis, and weather forecasting, helping make informed decisions based on probabilistic analysis.
